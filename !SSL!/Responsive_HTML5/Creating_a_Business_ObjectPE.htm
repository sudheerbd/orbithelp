Practice Example: Creating a Business Object

To create a new Business Object

 

Once the Logical model is created, create a Business Object. As learnt earlier, a business object is an intermediary between the domain and the reports.

 

To create a New Business Object

 

1. Under Logical Models, right-click the Business Objects, and click New Business Object.                                                                        

 

2.  New Business Object dialog box appears.

 

3. To create a New Business Object, perform the following:

 

·   In the Name box, type the name of the business object.

 

? Example: BO Name: Sample Business Object

 

·   In the Description box, type the description for the business object.

 

      ?   Example: Description: Description for Sample Business Object

 

·   To save the business object, click Save.

 

4.  Newly created business object appears under Business Objects in the Domain Hierarchy.

                  

 5.  The newly created business object opens two sections – Logical Tables <Business Object Name> and Business Objects  – appear in the work area. 

 

6.      Under Business Objects, right-click the newly created business objects, and then click New Category.

 

?   Example: Newly Created BO: Sample Business Object

 

7.      New Category option appears underneath the Business Object.

             

 

¥     Categories are created to organize the reports/objects.

 

8.      To group the related columns under one category, drag the required column from the Logical tables on the left hand-side, and drop them in the related categories.

 

?   Example: Related Category: Invoices

           

               

    9.     To save the changes, click Save.
